Benefits of Native Plants

Support Pollinators

Native plants provide essential food and habitat for bees, butterflies, and other pollinators.

Conserve Water

Once established, native plants typically require less water than non-native species.

Low Maintenance

Native plants are adapted to local conditions and require less fertilizer and pesticides.

Wildlife Habitat

Provide shelter and food for birds, insects, and other local wildlife.
